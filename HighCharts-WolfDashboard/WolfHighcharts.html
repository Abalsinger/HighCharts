Details of the Wolf Population of Yellowstone Since 1995 Reintroduction

Although the gray wolves once roamed from Northern Canada to Mexico throughout the 19th century they were regarded as a dangerous nuisance. The gradual loss of habitat and deliberate extermination programs led to their disappearance throughout most of the United States. By 1926, when the National Park Service ended its predator control efforts, there were no gray wolf packs left in Yellowstone National Park.In the decades that followed, the importance of the wolf as part of a healthy functioning ecosystem came to be better understood, and the gray wolf was eventually listed as an endangered species in 1978. In the 1980s the US National Park Service called for the restoration of the gray wolf to the Yellowstone Park area. Efforts culumiated in the release of fourteen wolves in 1995 and 17 wolves in 1996 were translocated to Yellowstone. Since then yearly population reports are made in a effort to monitor reintroduction efforts.
